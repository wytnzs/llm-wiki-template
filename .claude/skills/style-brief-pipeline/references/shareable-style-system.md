# Shareable Style System

Use this reference when the output will be shared with other creators, teams, or the public.

## Principle

Share the system, not the owner's writing personality.

Public materials should teach people how to define their own voice and visual language. They should not cause everyone to sound or look like the owner.

## What To Include

Shareable specs may include:

- style selectors
- design variables
- content routing rules
- platform layout principles
- component categories
- image prompt structures
- QA checklists
- blank self-definition worksheets

## What To Remove

Do not include:

- the owner's exact opening habits
- private tone rules
- personal judgment shortcuts
- recurring phrases
- private audience assumptions
- unpublished examples
- private knowledge cards or case materials

## Shareable Style Selector

Let users choose or define:

| Dimension | Options |
|---|---|
| Voice strength | gentle / analytical / sharp / narrative |
| Visual density | airy / balanced / dense |
| Color behavior | monochrome / single accent / editorial contrast |
| Platform priority | long-form reading / social sharing / conversion |
| Image role | decorative / explanatory / data-first |
| Trust posture | personal experience / professional analysis / evidence-led |

## Shareable Output Pattern

When creating a public template, provide:

1. A neutral explanation of the method.
2. A blank `style-brief` schema.
3. Three example routes with generic placeholders.
4. A checklist for personalization.
5. A warning not to copy another creator's voice wholesale.

## Public Language

Use wording like:

- "Define your voice strength."
- "Choose one mother style and two routed variants."
- "Write your own forbidden expressions."
- "Use this field to record your audience relationship."

Avoid wording like:

- "Use my tone."
- "Write like me."
- "Copy this exact opening."
- "Apply my content personality."
